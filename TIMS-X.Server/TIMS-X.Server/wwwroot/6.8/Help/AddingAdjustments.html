    Adding Adjustments

    Claim adjustments can be added for Primary Payers, Secondary through Quinary Payers, and Patients.  Payer adjustments will usually be entered as they appear on the remittance notice received for the claim.  The amounts and codes will be obtained from that document.  Patient (or Other) adjustments can be used for miscellaneous entries that don't originate from the payer, such as Error Corrections, Write-Offs, etc.  

    Note: Entering adjustments can be done at the same time you enter payments, so that all information from an EOB (remittance advice) can be entered at once.


    To add an adjustment follow these steps:

    1. Select the desired claim on the patient tree and click the Reg button in the Apply section at the bottom of the Adjudication screen.  

    The Apply Payments/Adjustments screen will popup.  To see the transaction lines on the top, click the Flip Display button .

    1. In the Select Payment Source section, select either the Payer, or Patient as the payment source. (required)

    1. Choose the Adjustment Type from the drop down field.  For an adjustment being entered from an EOB, select "Insurance Adjustment".  (required)

    1. The Date will default to today's date.  Choose another date if necessary. (required)
    2. If you are working off an EOB, it will be easier to complete the adjustment details for each line before going to the next one.
    3. Enter the Allowable amount if known in the Allowable column of the transaction list. Entering an allowable amount generally coincides with a payer adjustment of code CO-45.  To automatically enter the CO-45 adjustment click the right arrow button after the code "CO-45".  TIMS will subtract the Allowable amount from the Charge amount, and enter the difference into the Adjustment field. (see example)  

    You can also enter the adjustment amount directly into the field.

    1. Enter the Deductible and/or Co-Insurance amount if known. Note: Entering Allowable, Deductible and Co-Insurance amounts will not change the balance of the claim, however they are required for submitting the claim electronically to an additional payer.
    2. For adjustments other than CO-45 choose the appropriate CAS Group and enter the CAS Code directly. For most standard CAS codes, you can see a description of the code by holding your mouse cursor over the code field.    

    1. A CAS Group and CAS code is only required if the adjustment is from the primary or other payer.  They are not required if the Payment Source is the patient.  Enter the adjustment amount in the Adjustment field.
    2. Repeat the procedure for each transaction line.
    3. As you enter adjustment amounts for each line item, the Claim Balance will reflect the applied adjustments.

    1. When all the adjustments have been entered, click Save, or Cancel to abandon your changes.
    2. The adjustment will now be listed with the amount entered under each transaction line. 

    Notes about Adjustments:

    When a claim is resubmitted to a Secondary payer, any Primary payer adjustments and payments will be included along with the claim.  That is why the Adjustment Group and Code is a requirement for insurance adjustments.  Secondary payers require this information to know how the primary payer adjudicated the claim. In addition, the primary Deductible and Co-Insurance amounts are also sent with any secondary claims. This information can also be found on the remittance advice (EOB).

    Changing or Deleting an Adjustment

    If an adjustment has not been exported to QuickBooks® or included in a finalized GL Batch yet, you can edit the details or delete the adjustment entirely.

    1. Double-click on the adjustment line in the Transaction Details section. 

    The Edit Adjustment screen will popup.  "QuickBooks Status" will display "Not Synced".

    1. Edit any of the adjustment details, and click OK to save, or Cancel to abandon your changes.
    2. To delete the adjustment click the Delete button, a message will ask you to confirm the deletion.  Click Yes to delete, or No to cancel.
    3. Note: if the adjustment has been exported to QuickBooks or finalized in GL, you can still double-click on the adjustment line to bring up the Edit Adjustment screen.  However, the only fields that will be editable is the CAS Code field.  The export information will be displayed after "QuickBooks Status".

    Reversing Adjustments

    A negative adjustment (reversal) can be entered to correct an adjustment entered in error (for a wrong patient for example).  This would generally be done when the prior adjustment has already been exported to QuickBooks or finalized in GL and cannot be edited or deleted.  To add a negative adjustment follow these steps:

    1. Select the desired claim on the patient tree and click the Reg button in the Apply Payments/Adjustments section at the bottom of the Adjudication screen.  The Apply Payments/Adjustments screen will popup.  The fields for the payment and adjustment details will be on the top, and a list of all transactions in the claim will be at the bottom.
    2. In the Select Payment Source section, choose either the Primary Payer, Secondary Payer, or Patient as the payment source. Choose the same source as the adjustment you want to reverse.
    3. Choose the Adjustment Type from the drop down field.  Choose the same type as the adjustment you want to reverse.  
    4. The Date will default to today's date.  Choose another date if necessary.
    5. Enter the same CAS Group and CAS Code as the adjustment you want to reverse. (if the source is the primary or secondary payer)
    6. In the Adjustment column, enter the negative amount to be applied to each of the transaction lines. Enter a minus sign in front of the adjustment amount.  (see example).  

    As you enter amounts for each line item,  the Claim Balance will reflect the applied negative adjustment.

    1. Repeat the procedure for each transaction line you need to correct.
    2. When the payment has been completely distributed, click Save, or Cancel to abandon your changes.  A message will ask if you are sure you wish to post a negative adjustment, click Yes to save.

    1. The adjustment will now be listed with the applied amount under each transaction line with a negative adjustment amount.

    1. When a negative adjustment (reversal) is entered, it will be exported as a line item to the original invoice in QuickBooks .  It will appear in the original invoice as an Item Code "Insurance Adjustment" or "Patient Fees - Adjustment". The line item will be posted as a positive amount to the accounts assigned to Insurance Adjustments or Patient Fees-Adjustments on the date of the original invoice. (see QuickBooks instructions)
