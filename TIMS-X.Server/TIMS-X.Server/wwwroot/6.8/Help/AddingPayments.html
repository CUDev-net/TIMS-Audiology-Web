    Adding Payments

    Claim payments can be added for Primary Payers, Secondary Payers, and Patients.  The process is similar for all, with the exception of POS CoPayments, as discussed below.   

    Note: Entering payments can be done at the same time you enter adjustments, so that all information from an EOB can be entered at once.

    Add a Payment

    1. Select the desired claim on the patient tree and click the Reg button in the Apply section at the bottom of the Adjudication screen.

    The Apply Payments/Adjustments screen will popup.  The fields for the payment and adjustment details will be on the top, and a list of all transactions in the claim will be at the bottom. To see the transaction lines on the top, click the Flip Display button .

    1. In the Select Payment Source section, choose either the Payer, or Patient as the payment source. (required)

    1. In the Payment Options section, select "Manual" to record the payment in TIMS and process elsewhere.  Select "Credit Card" or "E-Check" to process either type directly through TIMS.  See E-Payment Processing for more information. The Refund Check option is used when a refund needs to be issued to the patient - see Refund Checks below.

    1. Choose the Payment Method from the drop down field. (required) Payments must be previously added in Setup.

    1. The Date will default to today's date.  Choose another date if necessary. (required)
    2. Enter the Reference Number for the transaction. (required)
    3. In the Payment column of the transaction list, enter the amount to be applied to each of the transaction lines.  (see example).  

    As you enter amounts for each line item, the Payment Balance field will be updated with the total payment amount, and the Claim Balance will reflect the applied payment.

    1. When the payment has been completely distributed, click Save, or Cancel to abandon your changes.
    2. The payment will now be listed with the applied amount under each transaction line. (see example)

    Changing or Deleting a Payment

    If a payment has not been exported to QuickBooks® or included in a finalized GL Batch yet, you can edit the details or delete the payment entirely.

    1. Double-click on the payment line in the Transaction Details section. 

    1. The Edit Payment screen will popup.  "QuickBooks Status" will display "Not Synced".

    1. Edit any of the payment details, and click OK to save, or Cancel to abandon your changes.
    2. To delete the payment, click the Delete button, a message will ask you to confirm the deletion.  Click Yes to delete, or No to cancel. Note: This will only delete the part of the payment applied to the particular line item, not the entire payment.
    3. Click on the Payments button to see a summary of the total payment for all lines in the claim.

    If the payment is from the patient, you can select that line and click the Print Pmt Receipt button to view and print a receipt if needed.

    1. Note: if the payment has been exported to QuickBooks® or finalized in GL, you can still double-click on the payment line to bring up the Edit Payment screen.  However, the fields will not be editable and the export information will be displayed after "QuickBooks Status".

    POS CoPayments

    In the POS Module, you can designate a payment receipt as an Insurance CoPayment and link it to a specific appointment, which can then be generated into a claim.  If the claim you are viewing has a copayment linked to it,  the patient name and claim in the Patient Tree will be green.  

    In addition, the Copay button in the Apply Payments/Adjustments section will be active and blue.  Follow these steps to complete the process and apply the co-payment to the claim.


    1. Click the Copay button . The Apply Patient CoPayments screen will popup.

    1. The CoPayment amount, payment method, reference and date will automatically be filled in with the data originally entered when the POS payment was made.

    1. The transaction lines will be listed and the only available fields for editing are the Payment fields for each line.
    2. Distribute the payment across one or more of the transaction lines.  As you enter amounts, the Payment Balance will update to the total distributed so far.

    1. When you are finished distributing the co-payment, click Save.  If you did not distribute the entire payment, a message will popup letting you know what the remaining balance is.  Click Yes to save without distributing the entire payment, or No to return to the previous screen and distribute the remaining amount. You can return later to distribute the remaining amount if necessary.

    1. The payment will now be listed with the applied amount under each transaction line.

    1. Note: Co-payment details cannot be edited in Adjudication once they have been distributed. However, they can be removed using the Remove CoPays function from the CoPay Adjustment screen in the POS Payment. (see Adjusting CoPayments in POS help)

    Reversing Payments

    A negative payment (reversal) can be entered to correct a payment entered in error (for a wrong patient for example).  This would generally be done when the prior payment has already been exported to QuickBooks  and cannot be edited or deleted.  To add a negative payment follow these steps:


    1. Select the desired claim on the patient tree and click the Reg button in the Apply Payments/Adjustments section at the bottom of the Adjudication link.  The Apply Payments/Adjustments screen will popup.  The fields for the payment and adjustment details will be on the top, and a list of all transactions in the claim will be at the bottom.

    1. In the Select Payment Source section, choose either the desired Payer or Patient as the payment source. (required)
    2. In the Payment Options section, select "Manual" to record the payment in TIMS and process elsewhere. Select "Credit Card" or "E-Check" to process either type directly through TIMS.  See E-Payment Processing for more information. The Refund Check option is used when a refund needs to be issued to the patient - see Refund Checks below.

    1. Choose the Payment Method from the drop down field.  (required)

    1. The Date will default to today's date.  Choose another date if necessary. (required)
    2. Enter the Reference Number for the transaction. (required)
    3. In the Payment column of the transaction list, enter the negative amount to be applied to each of the transaction lines.  Enter a minus sign in front of the payment amount.   

    As you enter amounts for each line item, the Payment Balance field will be updated with the total negative payment amount, and the Claim Balance will reflect the applied negative payment.

    1. When the payment has been completely distributed, click Save, or Cancel to abandon your changes.  A message will ask if you are sure you wish to post a negative payment, click Yes to save.

    1. The payment will now be listed with the applied amount under each transaction line with a negative payment amount. 

    1. It will be exported as a General Journal Entry in QuickBooks  and will appear in the Patient Register as type "General Journal", dated as the date of export from TIMS. (see QuickBooks instructions)

    Refund Checks

    If there is a need to issue the patient a refund check because of an over payment, it can be done with the same steps above for a Payment Reversal, with the following changes:

    1. In the Payment Options section, select Refund Check.

    1. The Payment Method choices will only be those from Refund Check Accounts previously entered in Setup. (required). Choose the appropriate method/account.

    1. In the Payment column of the transaction list, enter the negative amount to be applied to each of the transaction lines.  Enter a minus sign in front of the payment amount.

    1. When the payment has been completely distributed, click Save, or Cancel to abandon your changes.  A message will ask if you are sure you wish to post a negative payment, click Yes to save.

    1. Another message will ask you to confirm the negative payment amount. Click OK to save.
