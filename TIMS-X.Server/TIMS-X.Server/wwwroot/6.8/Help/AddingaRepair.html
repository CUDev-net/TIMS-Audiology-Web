    Adding a Repair

    To add a new Repair, follow the steps outlined below.  Refer to the overview section of Repairs for specifics on each field.


    1. Click the Add New Repair icon.  Then Repair window will popup.  If the current date is greater than the Warranty Date of the hearing aid, then the Payable box will be checked by default.

    1. If the Payable box is checked, the Repair Item field will display.  Select the POS repair item to be billed (if other than "Repair").
    2. The Repair Order Number will be automatically generated when the repair ticket is saved.
    3. The Received from Patient date will default to the date that repair ticket is created and can be changed as needed. (required field)
    4. Use the Complaint drop down menu to choose the reason that a repair is needed. 
    5. If applicable, enter the date that the repair is sent to the manufacturer.
    6. Enter a date in Due Back.  (required field)
    7. Enter the Shipping Number, if applicable, and click OK.
    8. When a repair ticket has been created it will be indicated as an Outstanding Repairs and L&D in orange in the hearing aid record and can be tracked using the Hearing Aid Status report.

    1. When the repair is returned from the manufacturer, edit it by double clicking on the repair date listed. 

    Enter the date that it comes Back from Repair, the manufacturer invoice number and Action taken by the manufacturer.

    1. If the hearing aid is replaced rather than repaired check the Replacement Received or L&D Replacement Received checkbox and enter the Replacement Serial Number, UDI Number, and the Repair Warranty Date if applicable.

      1. When a Repair Warranty Date is entered, a popup message will ask if you wish to replace the hearing aid Current Warranty Date with the Repair Warranty Date. Click Yes to replace, or No to leave the Current Warranty Date as is.

    1. Enter the Returned to Patient date to indicate that the repair is complete in the hearing aid record and on the Hearing Aid Status report. 
    2. When a replacement hearing aid is entered in the repair ticket and the ticket is saved, the status for the hearing aid that was replaced is automatically changed to Repair Replaced.  It will appear as an "active" device in the HA History screen until the Returned to Patient date is filled in.

    1. A note is automatically created in the hearing record to document that the hearing aid was replaced and referenced by the repair order number.

     

    1. When a hearing aid is replaced a new hearing aid record is automatically created for the replacement hearing aid with a status of In Use – Repair Replacement or In Use - L&D Replacement, and the new serial number. 

    1. A note is automatically created in the hearing record to document that the hearing aid is a repair replacement and the serial number for the hearing aid that was replaced is referenced.
