    Adjudication Overview

    Adjudication is where the judgment from the clearinghouse and payers is displayed about the submitted claims. Once the claim has been submitted to the clearinghouse, it returns here to display the status and can be processed further, resubmitted, printed, and/or completed.

    Patient Tree

    The Patient Tree shows patients who have claims that have been submitted. 

    Click on the arrow next to the patient's name to expand the tree and display their claim(s).

    The display will show the Claim Number, followed by the Batch Transmission Date. Holding the mouse cursor over the claim will show a popup tooltip with the Date(s) of Service of the claim. Click on the claim to display the details of the claim to the right.  From here it can be processed further, resubmitted, printed, completed, or recovered (if already completed).


    The list can be filtered to show claims from certain locations only by clicking the Site drop down field.  Patient names are color-coded according to the following criteria if the Colorize Button is clicked in "All" mode. 


    • Black - claim has been accepted by the clearinghouse
    • Red - claim has been rejected by the clearinghouse
    • Green - there are pending POS co-payments that need to be applied to the claim
    • Blue - there are pending ERN transactions that need to be applied to the claim
    • Purple - there are ERN transactions that indicate a payer rejection
    • Maroon - claim has been accepted by the clearinghouse, but later rejected by the Payer on a subsequent notice


    The list can also be filtered to show claims from a selected insurance carrier by clicking the Payer drop down field.

    Modes

    There are also multiple Modes available to display claims from various categories.

    • All -  this is the default mode displayed when accessing Adjudication for the first time in a login session. This mode displays all pending claims that have been submitted and have had a response received from the clearinghouse (either accepted or rejected).
    • Clearinghouse Rejects - Displays all pending claims that have been submitted and have been rejected by the clearinghouse. Patient names and claims that have been rejected will be red.
    • Payer Rejects - Displays all pending claims that have been submitted, accepted by the clearinghouse, but rejected by the payer on a subsequent notice.  Payer rejects status will look at the last claim status response received. Patient names and claims that have been rejected by the payer will be in maroon.
    • ERN Trans - claims that have received an Electronic Remittance notice.  Patient names and claims that have outstanding ERN transactions will be in blue.
    • POS CoPays - Displays all pending claims where a POS Co-Payment has been posted to the date of service and has not yet been applied to the claim.  Patient names and claims that have outstanding co-payments will be in green.
    • Selected Patient - This mode displays all claims for the selected patient, both pending and completed. Search for patient by typing in the Search box. As soon as you type 3 characters, the search criteria boxes will be displayed.  You can search by patient last name, first name, or claim number.  Enter the name or claim number, then click the Search button (or hit Enter key). Search results will include patients with Last or First name containing the characters entered, or claim number exactly matching what was entered. Resulting patients must have a claim that would otherwise appear in Adjudication (either pending or complete).  If there are any matches, search results will appear below. 

    Double-click on the patient's name to expand the tree and display all their claim(s). Completed claims will be in bold font.  

    Click on the claim to display the details of the claim to the right.  From here it can be processed further, resubmitted, printed, completed, or recovered (if already completed).

    • Waiting Payer - Displays all pending claims that have been submitted electronically and have not had an electronic response received yet from the payer. (Based on Payer Notes - see below). Claims that have been submitted 10 or fewer days ago will not be included.
    • All (Negative Balance) - Displays all pending claims that have been submitted and have had a response received from the clearinghouse, and have a negative (credit) balance. This could result from an over-payment by the payer or patient.
    • All (Zero Balance) - Displays all pending claims that have been submitted and have had a response received from the clearinghouse, and have a zero balance due. When switching to this mode, an additional icon will appear next to the Mode box.   Clicking this button allows you to automatically complete all the zero balance claims.   On the message that pops up, click Yes to complete all, or No to cancel.

    • All (0-30 Days Past) - Displays all patients with pending claims that have been submitted between 0 and 30 days ago, and have had a response received from the clearinghouse.
    • All (31-60 Days Past) - Displays all patients with pending claims that have been submitted between 31 and 60 days ago, and have had a response received from the clearinghouse.
    • All (61-90 Days Past) - Displays all patients with pending claims that have been submitted between 61 and 90 days ago, and have had a response received from the clearinghouse.
    • All (90+ Days Past) - Displays all patients with pending claims that have been submitted more than 90 days ago, and have had a response received from the clearinghouse.
    • Attachments - Displays all pending claims that had claim attachment information entered with the claim before it was transmitted, or a manually added Attachment User Note line- see below. If an attachment has not yet been added, then the patient name and claim will be in purple font.
    • Awaiting Response - Displays all pending claims that have been submitted and have not had a response received yet from the clearinghouse.  This list should match the claims shown in Processing link that have been submitted.  These claims cannot be edited, however TIMS Support staff can perform certain actions on them if necessary. This can include requesting a re-queue of clearinghouse reports, resubmitting the entire batch, or manually marking the claims as accepted or rejected if reports are not available.  Please contact Support for more information.
    • Completed (All) - Displays all completed claims in the database. 
    • Completed (1 Mo) - Displays completed claims that were last transmitted up to one month ago.
    • Completed (3 Mo) - Displays completed claims that were last transmitted up to three months ago.
    • Completed (6 Mo) - Displays completed claims that were last transmitted up to six months ago.
    • Completed (1 Yr) - Displays completed claims that were last transmitted up to one year ago.
    • Completed Open ERN - Displays all claims that were completed with existing open ERN transactions. These claims should be un-completed, so that the ERN transactions can either be posted or voided. Patient names and claims that have outstanding ERN transactions will be in blue.
    • Completed Open CoPay -  Displays all completed claims where a POS Co-Payment has been posted to the date of service and has not yet been applied to the claim. Patient names and claims that have outstanding co-payments will be in green.
    • Completed Unsynced - Displays claims that have been completed but contain elements (transactions, payments, and/or adjustments) that have not synced to QuickBooks.  Click the Recover button to attempt to sync again.
    • Completed NonZero - Displays all completed claims that have a balance greater than zero.
    • ERN Rejects - claims that have received an Electronic Remittance notice, and have been rejected by the payer.  Patient names and claims that have outstanding ERN transactions will be in purple.
    • In Collection - Displays claims that have been marked for collection by entering a date in Collection Date field.

    • Manual Claims - Displays claims that were manually moved from the Claims-Processing link, and were not actually submitted to the clearinghouse.
    • Patient Responsibility - Displays all pending claims that have been adjudicated by all payers and/or are over 1 year old, and are now the patient's responsibility for payment.
    • Printed 1500 - Displays claims that have had a 1500 form printed. (see Printing a Claim Form for details)


    In any mode besides Selected Patient, double-clicking on the patient's name will switch you to Selected Patient mode for that patient, where you will then see all their claims, pending or completed).  Clicking on the arrow next to the patient name will expand the tree and show the patient's claims that are applicable for the current

    mode.

    The list of patient names and claims will refresh automatically if a new response file has been downloaded, co-payments have been applied, or the mode is changed.  Otherwise the list can be refreshed anytime by clicking the Refresh button .

    Claim Header

    This section displays information about the claim that was submitted. 

    To make more room on the screen, the claim header can be collapsed by clicking here:

    • Claim# - the ID of the submitted claim.  Hover the mouse pointer over the Claim# to see the associated Site.

     

    • Transmit - the date the claim was submitted to the clearinghouse
    • Batch - the ID of the Batch the claim was submitted with
    • Billed To - will display either "Primary", "Secondary", "Tertiary", "Quaternary" or "Quinary", depending on which payer was billed.
    • Printed - if a 1500 form was printed for this claim, the date last printed will appear here.
    • Collection - if a claim has been marked for collection, the date entered will appear here
    • All Payers/Patient - displays each payer on the claim, and the patient name.  The payer to whom the claim was submitted for payment will be in blue.
    • Edit Transaction - you can edit certain insured or claim information here
      • Insurance Payers - click here to edit the Insured information.  Any changes here will be reflected in the Patient details, future claims, and/or a printed 1500 form.
      • Edit - There are 5 fields that can be edited here.  The Prior Authorization Number, Claim Frequency Code, and/or Payer Claim Control Number can be entered and will carry forward if the claim is resubmitted. Information can be typed that will appear in Box 19 (Reserved for Local Use) on a printed 1500 form. The Referring Provider can be changed/added here and will carry forward if the claim is resubmitted.  It can also be copied to the patient information if desired.  The Bill To Provider will be displayed (but is not editable)
        • Tracking - click to see audit tracking information about the claim, such as which user created and/or last updated it, from which site and when.
      • Codes - a read-only popup reference that displays information for status codes received from payers.
      • Collection - if the balance of the claim is past due and you wish to designate that it has been forwarded to a collection agency, click this button.  

    Enter a date that the claim was sent to collection, and any notes if desired.  The collection date and notes will automatically appear in the Payer Notes section. 

      • Rollover - click this button to add a note to the Payer Notes that indicates the claim was automatically rolled over to a secondary payer.  This is for recording information you may have received from the Clearinghouse or primary payer.
      • Status - if the practice has been enrolled in the service, click this button to check the claim status with the payer.  The response will be recorded in the Payer Notes section. (fees applicable - contact TIMS Support at 800-763-0308 for details)
    • Clearinghouse Status - the status of the claim after a response was received from the clearinghouse. If the claim was manually transferred from Claim Processing, and not submitted to the clearinghouse this will display "Manual Claim". Note - this is not the status from the actual payer, see Payer Notes below.  

    Clearinghouse Notes

    This section displays the notes and errors sent back from the clearinghouse regarding the claim. 

    These notes and errors are to be used by the claims transmitter to correct any problems with the claim before resubmitting. If there are multiple transaction lines on the claim, there will be separate notes for each line sequence number. Use the scroll bar to the right to see all the notes.  See Clearinghouse Errors for more details and examples of possible errors.  This field is editable - additional manual notes about the claim can be entered as needed, however it is recommended to add these the Payer and User Notes section. 

    Transaction Line Details

    This ledger-style section displays the details of the claim lines and any payments and adjustments that have been posted towards them.  

    Double-click on a line to view more details about the transaction and/or edit if allowed.  See "Working in Adjudication" for more details.

    • # - sequence number of the transaction line - the order in which the transaction lines were entered on the claim.
    • Description - depending on the entry-type, the Description will describe one of the following:
      • The procedure code and procedure description of the transaction line.  If the entire description is not visible, hold your mouse cursor over the field for a tool tip with a full description.

      • Payment method, reference, and payer for an entered payment. If the entire description is not visible, hold your mouse cursor over the field for a tool tip with a full description.

      • Payer, adjustment type, and adjustment code for an entered adjustment.  If the entire description is not visible, hold your mouse cursor over the field for a tool tip with a full description.

    • Balance - (Line Totals:)  the balance owed on the transaction line, calculated by Charges - (Payments + Adjustments).
    • Diagnosis - the first diagnosis code associated with the transaction.  If more than one code was submitted, the rest can be viewed by holding your cursor over the field for a tool tip.

    • Date - depending on the entry-type, the Date will describe one of the following:
      • The Date of Service for the transaction
      • The date the payment or adjustment was made
    • Charge - the original charge amount submitted for the transaction.
    • Adjust - the amount of the adjustment entered.
    • Payment - the amount of the payment entered.
    • Allowed - the maximum amount determined by the payer as being "allowable" under the provisions of the contract prior to the determination of actual payment.  This can generally be obtained from the primary payer EOB.
    • Deduct - The patient's deductible amount on this transaction.  This can generally be obtained from the primary payer EOB.
    • Co-Ins - The patient's co-insurance amount on this transaction.  This can generally be obtained from the primary payer EOB.
    • PRAmt - the amount of any PR (Patient Responsibility) adjustment entered.  PR adjustments do not affect the balance due for the claim. 
    • QB - if the claim, payment, and/or adjustment has been exported to QuickBooks , this box will be checked. Hold your cursor over the check box to see a tool tip of the export details, such as the date exported and corresponding QuickBooks  reference number.  

    This box is not editable, it will be automatically checked or un-checked depending on QB export status.

    • Select - this box will be checked by default. When a claim is resubmitted or completed, only the "Selected" transaction lines will be included in the process.  If you wish to resubmit or complete only certain lines, un-check this box for the lines you do not want to include.  To un-check the box, right click on the field and click on "Un-select".  To re-check the box, right click on the field and click on "Select".

    Payer and Office Notes


    • Payer and User Notes - this section will display claim status responses received from the payer, along with notes form the Clearinghouse. To make more room on the screen, this section can be collapsed, by clicking here:

     

    Note:  Not all payers will report acknowledgments and/or errors electronically. If the response contains keywords such as of “REJECT”, “RETURNED”,”FINAL/DENIAL”, and/or “ERROR”, the claim will be flagged as "Payer Reject" and appear in maroon font.  This section will also display notes automatically added by TIMS when edits are made to the insured/claim information, a 1500 form is printed, or the claim is marked for Collection.  You can scroll through all the notes by using the scroll bar to the right, or double-click anywhere in the section to open a window that displays all notes at once. 

    All of the notes can be printed by clicking the Print Screen button. If the transactions are resubmitted to either the primary or secondary payer, the Payer Notes will carry over to the new claim.  Notes originating from the payer cannot be edited, but User Notes can - see below.

      • User Notes - you can manually add a user Comment or Attachment note to the Payer Notes section. 

        • Comment -  Click Add Comment Line button . Choose the date, enter the note, then click the Save button. Checking the Red checkbox will manually set the claim to a "Payer Reject" status. 

        • User notes can be edited by selecting the line in the box above, editing the text then clicking the Save button. Delete a comment by selecting the line then clicking the Delete button.
        • Attachment - Adding an Attachment line will cause the claim to appear under the "Attachments" mode. You can use this to record an attachment that was sent to the payer outside of TIMS, such as a fax or by mail.  Click Add Attachment Line button . Choose the date, enter the note, then click the Save button. Attachment lines can be edited by selecting the line in the box above, editing the text then clicking the Save button. Delete an attachment line by selecting the line then clicking the Delete button.
    • Claim Notes - this field will display any notes that were entered in the "Remarks" in the Claims-Additional Claim Information section before the claim was submitted.  This field is editable, so any additional claim notes can be manually entered here.  If the transactions are resubmitted to either the primary or secondary payer, the Claim Notes will carry over to the new claim and be submitted.

    Claim Processing Actions

    This section is where you will initiate the various actions that can be entered or performed on the claim and claim lines.  See "Working in the Adjudication" for more details on each action.

    • Apply 
      • Reg - click here to enter payment, adjustment, allowable, deductible, and co-insurance information for each claim line.
      • CoPay - this button will only be active if there is an outstanding POS copayment available to apply to the claim. Click here to apply and distribute the payment.
      • CoPayHist - click this button to see details of the patient's POS payments, including copay amounts and whether they've been applied or not.
      • ERN - click this button to process ERN responses that have been received for this claim.
    • Transfer to Processing
      • Pri - If the claim has been rejected and you wish to correct the information and resubmit the claim for payment from the primary payer, click this button.  Only lines that have the "Select" box checked will be included.  For each line, a new transaction will be created in Processing - Claims. The lines will disappear from the Adjudication link and the new transaction(s) will be labeled as "Primary Resubmittal" on the Claims page.
      • Sec - If you wish to submit the claim electronically for payment from the secondary payer, click this button. Only lines that have the "Select" box checked will be included.  For each line, a new transaction will be created in Processing - Claims. The lines will disappear from the Adjudication link and the new transaction(s) will be labeled as "Secondary Resubmittal" on the Claims page.  This button will only be active if there is secondary payer listed in the Claim Header above.
      • Other - If you wish to submit the claim electronically for payment from the tertiary, quaternary, or quinary payer, click this button.  This button will only be active if additional payers beyond secondary are listed in the Claim Header above.
    • Finalize
      • Complete -  If the claim was accepted or you determine by the error that it can go no further, balance the claim and click this button. If the balance is not zero, a message will ask you if you are sure you wish to complete it.  

    Only lines that have the "Select" box checked will be included.

      • Recover - click this button to change a claim from Completed status back to pending. This will allow you to make further edits on the claim.  Only lines that have the "Select" box checked will be included. This button will only be active if the mode selected is one of the "Completed" ones.
    • ERNs
      • Process - click this button to process ERN (Electronic Remittance Notice) transactions. Note: You must already be enrolled with the clearinghouse for ERNs - contact TIMS Support for more information.
      • Check - click to see check information for previously received ERNs or other manual checks.
    • Printing
      • 1500 - click this button to print a 1500 claim form, to either the primary or secondary payer. See Printing a Claim Form for details.
      • UB04 - click this button to print a BU04 (institutional) claim form, to either the primary or secondary payer
      • Summary - click this button to print an EOB-style form, that includes charges, adjustments and payments made by the primary payer.  Can be used to send with a request for payment from a secondary or other payer.
      • INV - click this button to print a statement-style invoice for the selected claim, that shows all the charges, adjustments and payments.  Various options can be selected to print the invoice as desired. An option is also included to print invoices for all patients listed in Adjudication, and a #10 envelope as well.
    • Documents - click this button to attach a previously saved Patient Document to the claim.  If the practice is setup to do so, the attachment can be sent through TIMS - contact Support at 800-763-0308 for details. Attachments can be sent either Un-Solicited (not yet requested by payer) or Solicited.  If Solicited, you will most likely have a specific identification number supplied by the payer to use.  See Claim Attachment for more information.
