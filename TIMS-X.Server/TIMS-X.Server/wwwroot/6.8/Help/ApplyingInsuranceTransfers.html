    Applying Insurance Transfers

    Insurance Transfer documents will be exported as a General Journal entry with a negative amount, and can applied to an invoice just like any other payment or credit. 


    This is an example of a POS invoice, POS Transfer, and Claims invoice exported to QuickBooks®.  The Insurance Transfer appears as the General Journal entry.


    To apply the transfer to an invoice (in this case we will apply to the POS invoice), follow these steps:


    1. Open the invoice you wish to apply the transfer to by double-clicking on the line.

    1. Click on the Apply Credits button on the open invoice.

    1. In the Available Credits section, select the Insurance Transfer line and click the check box.  Click Done to save.

    1. The Payments Applied and Balance Due amounts will be updated on the invoice.  Click Save & Close. 

    1. The Transfer amount has now been applied and the new Open Balance of the invoice and transfer will be reflected in the register.


    Apply Insurance Transfer Reversal


    A negative Insurance Transfer (reversal) will exported as a General Journal entry with a positive amount.


    This is an example of a POS invoice, POS Transfer, and Claims invoice exported to QuickBooks.  Later, an Insurance Transfer reversal was exported.  The original Insurance Transfer appears as the General Journal entry with a negative amount.  The Reversal appears as a positive amount.


    If the original transfer was already applied to an invoice, you must un-apply it in order to correct the open balance. To un-apply the transfer, follow these steps:


    1. Open the invoice by double-clicking on the line. 

    1. Click on the Apply Credits button on the open invoice.

    1. In the Previously Applied Credits section, uncheck the box next to the original Insurance Transfer line.  Click Done to save.

    1. The Payments Applied and Balance Due amounts will be updated on the invoice.  Click Save & Close. 

    1. The Transfer amount has now been un-applied and the new Open Balance of the invoice and transfer will be reflected in the register.
