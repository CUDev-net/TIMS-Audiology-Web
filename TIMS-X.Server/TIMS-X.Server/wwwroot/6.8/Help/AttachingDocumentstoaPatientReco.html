    Attaching Documents to a Patient Record

    Scanned documents can be associated to a patient's record.  If a scanner is attached to the computer, any document can be directly scanned in and linked to the patient.  If there is no scanner attached, previously scanned PDF or image files can be associated.  New in version 6.07 - practices whose databases are hosted with TIMS can now upload patient photos and insurance cards directly from their mobile device by scanning a QR code.

    Scanning a Document

    1. Click the Attach Document button to open the TIMS Document Capture System screen.
    2. The TIMS Document Capture System screen will popup.

    1. Choose the Document Type - it is important to choose this first so the correct resolution quality is used.

      1. Choose "Insurance Card" to upload an image of the insurance information that will be displayed in the Patient Insurance Editor screen.  If "Insurance Card" is selected, the Description field will be replaced by  "Front" and "Back" checkboxes.  You will also need to select the Payer Level in the "Slot" field.

    1. If there is more than one choice, Select the Scanner to be used. TIMS will only recognize a scanner with a TWAIN driver.

      1. Scanner settings can be changed by clicking the Settings icon.

      1. Select a different Page Size, if something other than the default is needed.

    1. Click the Scan button.  A message will indicate that the document is being scanned.

       

    1. Once the document is scanned in, you can view and/or alter the image if you wish by using the built in editing tools.
      1. Click the Zoom In or Out buttons to zoom incrementally.  Click the Zoom button to choose from standard views.

     

      1. If the document has multiple pages, you can navigate through the pages by clicking Previous, Next or entering the page number to jump to. You can also use the Page Display choices to change how the pages are viewed.

     

      1. Click the Edit Page icon to see a list of editing options.

      1. Click Crop to open a separate cropping window. With the mouse, select the region you wish to crop out of the image, then click the green Crop button at the bottom of the window.  Click Cancel to abandon and return to the original image.  The cropped region will now be the entire page of the image. Note - this cannot be undone once you click Crop. You will have to exit from the imaging window, abandon all changes, and start over.

      1. Rotate the image to the left or right, or flip by clicking any of these choices.

      1. If there are multiple pages in the scan, and you wish to remove any of them, scroll or select that page in the Page Thumbnails panel to the left.

    Then click Edit Page - Delete Page.  The selected page will be removed.


      1. If there are multiple pages in the scan, and you wish to split them into separate documents, click Edit Page - Split Pages. The Split Pages window will popup, with each page in a separate section.  Each page can be assigned it's own document type and description (required). The descriptions will default to "Page 1", "Page 2", etc - but can be changed to something more meaningful. Click the green Save button to save the separate pages, or Cancel to abandon changes.

    A confirmation message will popup, click Yes to save, No to cancel.

    1. Type a Description for the file.

    1. Click here see definitions of all Capture System icons.
    2. Click the Save and Close icon.

             

    Note: - once the image is saved to TIMS, the imaging screen will close.

    1. The document will now appear in the patient's Activity Panel under Imported Documents, under the Document Type selected. Double-click on the line to view or print the document.  You can also edit and change the document type or description if needed.  Right click on the line to modify the date if needed.

     

    Attaching an Existing Document to a Patient

    1. Click the Attach Document to Patient button to open the TIMS Document Capture System screen.
    2. The TIMS Document Capture System screen will popup.

    1. Click the Open Document button.

    1. Browse to the location where your PDF or image file is located, and select the file. See below for the types of files that can be used.  Select the desired document and click Open.

    1. Choose the Document Type.   

      1. Select "Patient Photo" to upload an image of the patient that will be displayed in the Patient Summary panel.
      2. Choose "Insurance Card" to upload an image of the insurance information that will be displayed in the Patient Insurance Editor screen.  If "Insurance Card" is selected, the Description field will be replaced by  "Front" and "Back" checkboxes.  You will also need to select the Payer Level.


    1. Once the document has been uploaded, you can alter the image if you wish by using the built in editing tools. Click the Edit Page icon to see a list of editing options. You can also delete or split the individual pages if desired.  See above for editing tips.

    1. Type a Description for the file. 

    1. Click the Save and Close icon.

             

      

    Note: - once the image is saved to TIMS, the imaging screen will close.

    1. The document will now appear in the patient's Activity tree under a category of the Imported Documents, then the Document Type selected. Double-click on the line to view, print, email, fax, or save the document as an external file. You can also edit and change the document type or description if needed. Right click on the line to modify the date if needed. If a document of type "Patient Image" is uploaded, the picture will be displayed in the Patient Summary panel (not under Patient Documents).

     


    Mobile Uploading of Photo or Insurance Card  

    From Patient Summary Panel

    1. Click the down arrow next to QR Code

      OR

      1. Click the Attach Document button to open the TIMS Document Capture System screen.
      2. Click the QR Code icon on the toolbar.

    1. A large QR Code will display on the screen.

    1. Using a QR scanner on your mobile device, scan the code on the screen.  This will produce a URL link.  Click on the link to go to the TIMS website.

    1. Enter your practice Office Code, then select the User and enter a Password.  Click Sign in button.

     

    1. The patient Name, DOB, and gender will be displayed on the Upload screen. This is where you will upload or take a photo of the patient and/or their insurance card.

    1. Under Patient Photo click the Choose File button . Under Insurance Card Front or Back, choose the desired Payer Slot (Primary, Secondary, etc) then click the Choose File button. On the Select an image screen, choose a recent photo or click Browse to find another one. 

    To take a picture, click Camera. 

    Take a photo of the patient or insurance card. If the photo is satisfactory click OK, otherwise click Cancel and try again.

     

    1. The insurance card photos can be rotated by clicking the right and left arrows under the image. 

    1. When all desired photos are uploaded click the Submit button.   You will receive a confirmation message, and see a Return to Patient button. Close the mobile application.

    1. Click Close on the QR code on the TIMS software screen. 
    2. After a refresh period, the Patient Summary panel will be updated with the new patient picture.


    From the Patient Insurance Editor screen


    1. Insurance information can be accessed by clicking the Insurance Payers button in the Patient Details, the Edit Insurance icon in the Patient Summary Panel, or the Insurance Payers button in the Claims Processing page.
    2. Click the down arrow next to Insurance Card Photos at the top of the screen.

    1. Click the Use Mobile Device icon .

    1. A large QR Code will display on the screen. 

    1. Proceed as above, steps 3 - 7.
    2. After a refresh period, the new insurance card images will appear in the Front and Back boxes under the selected Payer Slot.

     

    Click on the Front or Back window to see a larger view of the photo. Photos can also be viewed from the Patient Activity Panel under Patient Documents - Insurance Card.

    Inactivating an Attached Document

    If a document was accidentally attached to a wrong patient, or it is incorrect in some way - you can inactivate it so that it no longer appears in the patient's activity tree.  Follow these steps:


    1. Locate the document in the patient's Activity tree under a category of the Document Type that was previously saved.

    1. Double-click on the line to open the document.
    2. Click the Inactive check box. 

    1. Click Save.

     

    You will be required to enter a reason why the image is being made inactive. Enter the reason and click OK.

    The TIMS Document Capture System screen will close.

    1. The document will disappear from the patient's Activity tree.
    2. The inactive document can still be viewed, however by going to the Imaging Center.

    1. Locate the patient name, then expand the tree.  The inactive document will be listed under the original category, with a lighter colored icon to the left of the date. (see example below). Hover over the document to see a tooltip of the inactive reason. Double-click on the line to view it.


    Note: The Imaging Center is also the place to view Special Orders and Stock Item invoices, sorted by date, PO Number, or serial number.
