    Exporting Claim Payments to QuickBooks

    1. Enter any primary, secondary, other payer and patient payments as well as the check reference numbers and the date paid in Adjudication.  Payments will not export if the amount, payer and/or the date paid field is blank.
    2. After the QB Sync is run the payments will be exported to QuickBooks®, and applied to the original claim invoice. If the invoice has been deleted in QuickBooks, then the payments will be applied to the patient account instead.
    3. Claim payments will export the payment method in QuickBooks and have the payment details in the memo line. (example: "TIMS Claim ## payment Payer") The ref/check# and Site (class) will also be exported.

    1. Patient payments will also use a payment method, but will be identified as a patient payment in the memo line (example: "TIMS claim 1071 Patient Payment") The ref/check# and Site (class) will also be exported.

    Payment Reversals

    When a negative payment (reversal) is entered for a patient in Adjudication or the POS module, it will be exported as a General Journal Entry in QuickBooks.  It will appear in the Patient Register as type "General Journal", dated as the date of export from TIMS.

    The journal entry cannot be automatically applied by TIMS to the proper claim invoice, so it needs to be manually applied in QuickBooks.  Follow these steps to apply the journal entry:

    1. Right-click on the Claim Invoice, Choose View Transaction History.

    1. Double click on the original applied payment line(s).

      1. You will need to repeat this step for each payment line individually.
    1. The payment screen will show a check next to the claim invoice and you will now also see a line for the General Journal entry.  
    2. Uncheck the payment from the claim invoice and check the line for the General Journal entry.

      1. If there is still a balance left on the payment after applying to General Journal, then apply the rest to the invoice at this point.
    1. Save & Close the Payment screen.

    Refund Checks

    Depending on how the Refund Check payment method is setup in POS Setup, when a refund check is entered for a patient in the POS module, it will be exported as either a General Journal Entry or Check in QuickBooks .  


    Account Type is Other Liability


    The Refund Check will be exported as a General Journal Entry, and will appear similar to the Payment Reversal above.  

    The funds will be posted as a credit to the Other Liability account.

    When the refund check to the patient is written, use this same Other Liability account as the Expense account. 


    Account Type is Bank


    The Refund Check will be exported as a Check, with the funds already posted as deducted from the bank account.  The check can then be printed from QuickBooks.
