    Insurance CoPayment

    You can designate all or part of a POS payment as an Insurance CoPayment.  The payment will be linked to a specific appointment, which can then be carried over and applied to the claim generated.  This will allow the recording (and exporting to QuickBooks®) of a patient copayment before the claim is submitted.


    When the patient arrives for the appointment and pays a portion of the charges, you can immediately enter that payment in the POS module and record the transaction.  Later, after a claim has been generated, submitted, and received back from the clearinghouse, the copayment can be applied directly to it in the Adjudication link.  If the payment has already been exported to QuickBooks, it will not export again.

    Adjusting Copayments

    Copayment amounts can now be adjusted, after the payment has been finalized. Click the Adjust CoPay button to change the linked appointment and/or amount applied to it. The CoPay Adjustment screen will popup.

    • To remove the CoPay amount entirely and not link the payment to any appointment, un-check the "Payment applied to Claim?" box.
    • To adjust the Copay amount, you can either check one of the minimum or maximum amount boxes

     OR enter the amount directly in the New CoPay Amount box.

    The amount must be between any amount that's already been applied and the total copayment amount. In the example shown, $200 is the minimum amount because that's how much has already been applied.  $300 is the maximum amount because that is the total amount already designated as a copayment.

    • To link the CoPay to a different appointment, choose the new one from the Appointment drop-down field. If the chosen appointment already has a linked claim in adjudication, it will show the Claim number and balance, along with the appointment information.

    • To remove CoPay amount(s) that have already been applied in adjudication, click the Remove CoPays button .

    The Adjust CoPay process can be used to distribute a copayment to more than one appointment/claim. The example below is for a $300 payment distributed to different claims.

    1. To change the claim for all or some of the remaining $100, open the POS payment and click the Adjust CoPay button . 
    2. The CoPay Adjustment window will popup. 
    4. Leave the CoPay Amount at $300, but choose a different Appointment/Claim.
    5. Apply any portion of the remaining $100 to the new claim in adjudication.
    6. Repeat the process, choosing different claims until all of the copayment has been distributed. 


    Example 2

    A $300 copayment was made and assigned to an appointment on 8/7/2014 (Claim 887). $200 of that copayment has been applied to the claim in adjudication, leaving $100. The remaining $100 is now needed to be applied elsewhere, not to a claim.

    1. Open the POS payment and click the Adjust CoPay button . 
    2. Change the CoPay Amount to $200.
    3. The CoPayment Amount is now $200 and has been fully applied.  The remaining $100 is now available to be applied to a POS document.


    Note:  Once a copayment has been applied (posted) to a claim in Adjudication, it cannot be voided.  If it was applied to a claim by mistake, you must first use the Remove CoPays function to remove it from the claim. Then the payment can be voided.
