    Insurance Transfers

    TIMS offers a way to auto-invoice a hearing aid sale as a POS Invoice, and also bill the charges to an insurance payer without double-counting the sales.  An Insurance Transfer item is used to subtract a fixed amount from the invoice that will be billed to insurance as a claim.  Insurance Transfers are added as a line item directly on a POS Invoice, or as a separate document that is applied to the invoice.

    Adding Insurance Transfer Item Lines


    Note: The invoice must have a previous appointment selected in the Document Information section in order to enter insurance billing information.

    1. Double-click on the line item, or highlight the item an click the Edit button .  The Selected POS Line window will popup.

    1. Click the down arrow next to Expand to Enter Billing Codes for Insurance Transfer.
    2. Click the Enter Billing Codes button . The CPT and Diagnosis Code selection screen will popup.  

    1. Choose one CPT Code and up to four Diagnosis Codes that will be used when the item is billed to an insurance claim.  Up to four modifiers can be selected as well, but are optional. Click OK.  The details will be displayed on the Selected POS Line window.

    1. The Insurance Amount is the amount that will be billed to insurance. It will default to the amount of the item, but can be changed as needed. This can not be greater than the total Amount for the item.  This amount also cannot be changed when the claim transaction is created.
    2. Click OK to save. The Clm box will now be checked for that line, indicating that it will be billed to insurance. 

    1. Repeat the procedure for any other items on the invoice that will be billed to insurance.
    2. To remove the CPT and/or Diagnosis codes (eliminate the insurance transfer) - click on the Enter Billing Codes button again, and then click OK without selecting anything. A popup message will ask if you wish to clear all fields - click Yes.

    1. When all other details for the invoice have been completed, click the Final button . Click Yes to the finalize confirmation message.  The Process Insurance Message window will popup.  

    Listed will be all lines that will be billed to insurance, along with the cpt codes and amounts.  Click OK to continue finalizing. Clicking Cancel will return to the POS Invoice screen and the document will not be finalized.

    1. There will now be additional transfer lines added to the invoice - one for every line that had the Clm box checked.

    1. Corresponding Claim Transaction lines have now been created.  See POS Insurance Transfers in Claims help for information on further processing.

    Insurance Transfer Document

    After an invoice has been saved and finalized, an insurance transfer can still be applied to it by creating a separate Insurance Transfer document.  Create a new POS Document and choose the Insurance Transfer button.

    The Transfer Form looks similar to the Payment Receipt.  

    If you are exporting to QuickBooks®, the Transfer will be exported as a General Journal entry and can applied to an invoice just like any other payment or credit. See Applying Insurance Transfers in QuickBooks help for more information.


    Select Source of Document

    Click New to create a new Payment.  Click Open to select from a list of

    the patient's non-finalized payment receipts.  Click All to select from a list of

    all the patient's payment receipts (finalized or open, regular or reversals).

    Patient

    The name and active address of the selected patient.

    Payment from Name and Address

    Will default to the patient’s name and active address in the Patient details. If

    an address is entered in Name & Address for Invoicing - this will be the default used.

    All fields can be edited as desired, and will be saved with the invoice only.

    Payment Type

    Choose a type here, which will determine further processing after the payment receipt is finalized.  The default is Manual. 

    Document Information


     Transfer Date

    Date of the payment receipt. Will default to the current date, but can be changed as needed.

     Site

    This will default to the site specified in the Patient details, but can be changed as needed.

     Transfer Method

    This will default to "Insurance Transfer". This is a required field.

     Transfer Reference

    This will default to "Insurance Transfer", but a different reference can be entered if desired.   This is required field.

     Transfer Amount

    Total amount to be billed to insurance. Note: the amount entered for the Insurance Transfer must be the same amount as will be entered in the subsequent insurance claim for the item.  The amount cannot be changed later when the claim is created. This is required field.

     Rev

    Check this box if you wish to post this transfer as a reversal.  A negative transfer (reversal) can be entered to correct a transfer entered in error (For example, entered for a wrong patient, or an amount that should have been split into multiple transfers). This would generally be done when the prior transfer has already been exported to QuickBooks® and cannot be edited, deleted or voided. A transfer reversal cannot be applied to an invoice or an existing transfer.  It will appear in the QuickBooks Patient Register as type "General Journal". See Applying Insurance Transfer Reversals in QuickBooks help for more information.

    Appointment

    Applied Amount Remaining

    The amount of the transfer that has yet to be applied to an invoice.

    CPT and Diagnosis Code Information

    Click the Enter Billing Codes button . The CPT and Diagnosis Code selection screen will popup.  Choose one CPT Code and up to four Diagnosis Codes that will be used when the item is billed to an insurance claim. Click OK. The details will be displayed in this section.

    Office Notes

    Open field for internal notes only.  These will not be printed on the customer's receipt, or exported to QuickBooks®.

    Customer Message 

    Customer message that will print on the transfer. Messages can be added in Setup.

    Memo

    Open field for memo notes. These can be included on a printed document if desired, and will be exported to QuickBooks.

    Document Commands

    • Final - lock the transfer so that it can no longer be edited.  Only finalized transfers will be exported to QuickBooks.
    • Print - click once to open a preview window.  Click the print icon on the preview to print to printer.   Note: Printing an open POS transfer receipt will automatically finalize it.
    • Delete/Void - If the credit memo is still Open, this button will display "Delete".  If finalized, it will display "Void". Deleting a transfer will remove it entirely from the database.  This is only allowed with an open credit memo.  A finalized transfer can only be Voided.  A transfer that has been exported to QuickBooks cannot be deleted or voided.
    • Apply POS - click this button to apply the transfer to a finalized invoice. See Applying Payments for details.
    • Close - close the active transfer, but remain in the POS module.  
    • Exit - close the POS module entirely

    Billing the Transferred Amount to Insurance

    Once a transfer amount has been recorded in the POS Module, it must also be recorded as a claim transaction in order to balance the entire transaction and maintain the correct total sales figures.  The POS Document must be finalized before the transaction will be available for further processing in the Transaction link. See Transactions help for details.
