    Opportunity Tracking in Action

    The majority of Opportunity Tracking revolves around the Appointment Details.  To witness Opportunity Tracking in action, make an appointment by simply dragging and dropping a patient into a slot on the Appointment schedule.  In the example below, we will use the Appointment Details window for the patient David Copperfield:


    If you were to examine David Copperfield’s history and information, you would discover the following:  this is his first appointment, he has no Audiogram history, and there is no HA history on file (no current or expired hearing aids).  Because of these facts, Opportunity Tracking has flagged David Copperfield with the status type 2 Ears Aidable.  The complete list of Opportunity status types include:


    • 2 Ears Aidable
    • 1 Ear Aidable
    • Re-sell 2 Ears
    • Re-sell 1 Ear
    • No Opportunity
    • Tested Not Sold
    • Tested Not Sold 1 Ear
    • No Marketing
    • Current User

    Definitions

    The terminology “current” and “expired” are used with reference to hearing aids located in the HA History records.  As mentioned above, Opportunity Tracking compares the Purchase Date from the HA History record to the “Tracking Threshold” that is set in Practice setup.  This comparison determines whether a hearing aid is “current”, or “expired” (the default Tracking Threshold is set to 36 months). It should be noted that Opportunity Tracking only examines the purchase dates on the HA History records whose status type is set to “In Use”.  The other two status types examined, “On Order” and “Received”, automatically disqualify a patient as being an “opportunity” of any kind. 


    In addition, Opportunity tracking scans all HA History records to locate any hearing aids that are in use.  For example, if a patient has hearing aids for both ears, but the records for those hearing aids are split between different HA history records, Opportunity Tracking will locate the existing records for each hearing aid and label the patient with the appropriate status type.


    Which opportunity “status” is flagged/assigned ultimately depends upon a variety of variables, and different potential combinations of variables could potentially display the same status (see 1 Ear Aidable below for an example). 


    The list of examples (or potential scenarios) under each status type is not necessarily exhaustive.


    • 2 Ears Aidable – A patient will be flagged as 2 Ears Aidable if they are a new patient and have no previous appointment history.  Please consider:  in theory, a “new” patient should have no Audiograms on file.  If (for some reason) Audiograms do exist, Opportunity Tracking ignores these if the patient is new. A patient will also be flagged as 2 Ears Aidable, even if they have previous appointments, but have no hearing aids and no Audiogram information on file.
    • 1 Ear Aidable – If a patient has Audiograms for both ears, yet hearing loss in only one ear (Audiogram), and no hearing aid histories, they will be flagged as 1 Ear Aidable. If a patient has hearing loss in both ears (Audiogram) and only one ear with a hearing aid (current), they will be flagged as 1 Ear Aidable. If a patient has hearing loss in both ears (Audiogram), has no hearing aids, but has No Marketing attached to one of the ears, they will be flagged as 1 Ear Aidable.
    • Re-sell 2 Ears – When a patient’s hearing aids are expired for both ears (hearing aid histories that are not current, based on the threshold entered), the patient will be flagged as Re-sell 2 Ears. A patient will also be flagged as Re-sell 2 Ears if they have one hearing aid that is expired, as well as an Audiogram that demonstrates hearing loss in the unaided ear as well.
    • Re-sell 1 Ear – If a patient has hearing aids in both ears, and the device has expired for one ear (ex. left side), the patient will be flagged as Re-sell 1 Ear. A patient may also be flagged as Re-sell 1 Ear if they have expired hearing aids in both ears, but the patient has “No Marketing” set for one of the ears. A patient will be flagged as Re-sell 1 Ear if they have an expired hearing aid in one ear, and no hearing loss in the other.
    • No Opportunity – If a patient has no demonstrated hearing loss (via latest Audiogram), they will be flagged as No Opportunity when an appointment is made. If a patient has a current hearing aid in one ear, and the No Marketing 1 Ear restriction is enabled, this patient will be flagged as No Opportunity, regardless of the condition of the other ear (please see Communication Restrictions in the next section for further clarification).
    • Tested Not Sold If a patient’s most recent Audiogram records hearing loss for both the Right and Left ears, and the patient has no Hearing Aid histories (i.e. no hearing aid has been sold nor is currently in use) the patient will be flagged as Tested Not Sold. 
    • Tested Not Sold 1 Ear – If a patient’s most recent Audiogram records hearing loss for one ear, and the patient has no Hearing Aid histories (i.e. no hearing aid has been sold nor is currently in use) the patient will be flagged as Tested Not Sold 1 Ear. A patient will also be flagged as Tested Not Sold 1 Ear if they have Audiograms on file showing hearing loss for both ears, but only one hearing aid (HA History) for one ear.
    • No Marketing – A patient will be flagged as No Marketing if the related “No Marketing” communication restriction is enabled, regardless of the condition of either/both ears (i.e. whether there is hearing loss and/or outdated hearing aids for either ear).
    • Current User - If a patient has 2 hearing aids that are current, they will be flagged as Current User when an appointment is made. A patient will be flagged as Current User if they have a current hearing aid in one ear, and no demonstrated hearing loss (via Audiogram) in the other ear. 

    Communication Restrictions

    The two communication restrictions that impact Opportunity Tracking logic include “No Marketing” and “No Marketing 1 Ear”.  To better understand their impact upon Opportunity Tracking, consider each Communication Restriction below, and the associated scenario(s) that follow:


    • No Marketing - Patient A has any combination of hearing loss and/or outdated hearing aids in either/both ears.  This patient also has the “No Marketing” communication restriction checked.  Because of this restriction, and regardless of any other factor that Opportunity Tracking normally includes in its logic (HA History information, Noah Audiogram data, etc.), Patient A will always be flagged with the “No Marketing” status.
    • No Marketing 1 Ear - Patient B has hearing loss in only one ear, and/or has hearing loss in only one ear and an outdated hearing aid in the same ear.  This patient also has the “No Marketing 1 Ear” communication restriction checked.  Due to this restriction, and despite the fact that one ear is clearly a sales opportunity, Patient B will always be flagged with the “No Marketing” status.

    Opportunity Tracking in the History Link

    If the practice is setup to automatically create a history record for each appointment, the Opportunity Status for the appointment will also appear in the history record as a tool tip.  Hold the mouse over the Visit Date field to see the Opportunity Status, along with the associated appointment date and type.
