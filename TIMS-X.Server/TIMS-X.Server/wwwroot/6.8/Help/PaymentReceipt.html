  1. POS

    Payment Receipt

    Create a new Payment Receipt using the same steps as for Invoices and Credit Memos. Refer to the saving and printing information under the Invoice section for more clarity. You cannot finalize a payment with an amount of zero.


    See E-Payment Processing for processing credit, debit, and check payments directly through TIMS.


    Select Source of Document

    Click New to create a new Payment.  Click Open to select from a list of

    the patient's non-finalized payment receipts.  Click All to select from a list of

    all the patient's payment receipts (finalized or open, regular or reversals).

    Patient

    The name and active address of the selected patient.

    Payment from Name and Address

    Will default to the patient’s name and active address in the Patient details. If

    an address is entered in Name & Address for Invoicing - this will be the default used.

    All fields can be edited as desired, and will be saved with the invoice only.

    Payment Type

    Choose a type here, which will determine further processing after the payment receipt is finalized.  The default is Manual. 

    Document Information


     Payment Date

    Date of the payment receipt. Will default to the current date, but can be changed as needed.

     Site

    This will default to the site specified in the Patient details, but can be changed as needed.

     Payment Method

    Choose from the payment methods previously entered in Setup. This is a required field.

     Payment Reference

    Check or Reference number.  This is required field.

     Payment Amount

    Total amount of the payment.

     Rev

    Check this box if you wish to post this payment as a reversal (refund).  A negative payment (reversal) can be entered to correct a payment entered in error (for a wrong patient for example).  This would generally be done when the prior payment has already been exported to QuickBooks® and cannot be edited, deleted or voided. A payment reversal cannot be applied to an invoice or an existing payment.  It will appear in the QuickBooks  Patient Register as type "General Journal" and as a negative amount in the deposit report, dated as the date of export from TIMS. (see QuickBooks instructions for applying the reversal in QuickBooks)

     Applied Amount Remaining

    The amount of the payment that has yet to be applied to an invoice.

    Payment applied to Claim?

    Check this box if you wish to apply all or part of the payment to a current or future insurance claim generated in TIMS. (see below for more details)

     CoPayment Amount

    Enter the amount you wish to apply as a copayment.

     Appointment

    Choose the appointment that the co-payment applies to.

     CoPayment Applied

    If the copayment has already been applied in Adjudication, the amount applied will be shown here. Not editable.

     Applied to Claim No

    If the copayment has already been applied in Adjudication, the Claim it was applied to will be shown here. Not editable.

    Office Notes

    Open field for internal notes only.  These will not be printed on the customer's receipt, or exported to QuickBooks®.

    Customer Message 

    Customer message that will print on the invoice. Messages can be added in Setup.

    Memo

    Open field for memo notes. These can be included on a printed document if desired, and will be exported to QuickBooks.

    Document Commands

    • Final - lock the payment so that it can no longer be edited.  Only finalized credit memos will be exported to QuickBooks.
    • Print - click once to open a preview window.  Click the print icon on the preview to print to printer.   Note: Printing an open POS payment receipt will automatically finalize it.
    • Delete/Void - If the payment receipt is still Open, this button will display "Delete".  If finalized, it will display "Void". Deleting a payment receipt will remove it entirely from the database.  This is only allowed with an open payment receipt.  A finalized payment receipt can only be Voided.  A payment receipt that has been exported to QuickBooks cannot be deleted or voided.
    • Apply POS - click this button to apply the credit to a finalized invoice or payment reversal.
    • Adjust CoPay - This button will only appear when the payment has been finalized.  Click here to change or remove the amount and/or appointment that the copay will be applied to. (see Adjusting CoPayments)
    • Close - close the active payment receipt, but remain in the POS module.  
    • Exit - close the POS module entirely

    Insurance CoPayments

    You can designate all or part of a POS payment as an Insurance Copayment.  The payment will be linked to a specific appointment, which can then be carried over and applied to the claim generated.  This will allow the recording (and exporting to QuickBooks) of a patient copayment before the claim is submitted. See Insurance CoPayment help for more information.


    When the patient arrives for the appointment and pays a portion of the charges, you can immediately enter that payment in the POS module and record the transaction.  Later, after a claim has been generated, submitted, and received back from the clearinghouse, the copayment can be applied directly to it in the Adjudication link.  If the payment has already been exported to QuickBooks , it will not export again.
