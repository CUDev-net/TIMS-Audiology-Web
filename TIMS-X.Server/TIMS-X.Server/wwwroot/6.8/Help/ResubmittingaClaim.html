    Resubmitting a Claim

    Resubmit to Primary Payer

    If the claim has been rejected and you wish to correct the information and resubmit the claim for payment from the primary payer, click the Pri button in the Transfer to Processing section.

    A confirmation message will popup.  

    Note: If the patient's primary insurance has changed since the claim was submitted, the Primary dropdown field will have a choice of which payer you wish to resubmit this claim to.  It will default to the existing payer (the usual choice). But if you wish to switch to the patient's new current insurance, you can change it here.  If the patient's insurance has not been changed, there will be no choice on the drop down field.


    Only lines that have the "Select" box checked will be included.  There are 2 Options:

    • Transfer - all the selected lines will be resubmitted with a new claim automatically generated that retains the information from the previous claim.  A confirmation message will popup if the payer has been changed, click OK to continue. The new claim will appear in Processing - Claims Mode, with transactions labeled as "Pri".   

    If no other claim information needs to be changed, you can proceed directly to transmit the new claim.

    • Cancel - return to the Adjudication screen without resubmitting.

    For each resubmitted line, the lines will disappear from Adjudication and the new claim will appear in Processing - Claims Mode.

    Submit Electronically to Secondary Payer

    Once the remittance advice is received and the information is entered from the Primary payer, you can submit the claim electronically for payment from the secondary payer.  If the secondary payer was unknown at the time the claim was initially submitted, you can add it at this time by clicking the Insurance Payers button in the Edit Transaction section.  

    The Patient Insurance Editor will popup. Add or edit the secondary payer information as needed.

    Click the Sec button in the Transfer to Processing section.  

    Before submitting to Secondary insurance, the balance of Primary Payments, Primary Adjustments, Deductible and Co-Insurance must equal the line charges. If this is not the case, the message that pops up will let you know what the discrepancy amount is.  

    You will not be able to resubmit to Secondary until all selected lines are in balance. 

    Note: If the patient's secondary insurance has changed since the claim was submitted, the Secondary dropdown field will have a choice of which payer you wish to resubmit this claim to.  It will default to the existing payer (the usual choice). But if you wish to switch to the patient's new current insurance, you can change it here.  If the patient's insurance has not been changed, there will be no choice on the drop down field.

    If the claim is in balance, the confirmation message will have 2 Options:

    • Transfer - all the selected lines will be resubmitted with a new claim automatically generated that retains the information from the previous claim.  The new claim will appear in Processing - Claims Mode, with transactions labeled as "Sec".    

    If no other claim information needs to be changed, you can proceed directly to transmit the new claim.

    • Cancel - return to the Adjudication screen without resubmitting.

    For each resubmitted line, the lines will disappear from Adjudication and the new claim will appear in Processing - Claims Mode.

    Submit Electronically to Other Payers

    Once the remittance advice is received and the information is entered from the Primary and Secondary payers, you can submit the claim electronically for payment from additional payers, if there are any.  If the other payers were unknown at the time the claim was initially submitted, you can add them at this time by clicking the Insurance Payers button in the Edit Transaction section. The Patient Insurance Editor will popup.  Up to five insurance payers can be entered for the patient.


    Click the Click the Other button in the Transfer to Processing section. This button will only be active if the patient has more than two assigned payers.

    Once you click this button, the subsequent choices will depend on how many payers the patient has.  (Tertiary=3rd Payer, Quaternary=4th Payer, Quinary=5th Payer)

    Before submitting to another insurance,the net total to the Primary payer must be zero, and the net remainder of the claim must be greater than zero. If this is not the case, the message that pops up will let you know what the discrepancy amount is.  

    You will not be able to resubmit to the other payer until all selected lines are in balance.

    Note: If the patient's other insurance has changed since the claim was submitted, the corresponding dropdown field will have a choice of which payer you wish to resubmit this claim to.  It will default to the existing payer (the usual choice). But if you wish to switch to the patient's new current insurance, you can change it here.  If the patient's insurance has not been changed, there will be no choice on the drop down field.


    If the claim is in balance, the confirmation message will have 2 Options:

    • Transfer - all the selected lines will resubmitted with a new claim automatically generated that retains the information from the previous claim.  The new claim will appear in Processing - Claims Mode, with transactions labeled as "Ter" (or Quar/Quin).   

    If no other claim information needs to be changed, you can proceed directly to Processing to transmit the new claim.

    • Cancel - return to the Adjudication screen without resubmitting.

    For each resubmitted line, the lines will disappear from Adjudication and the new claim will appear in Processing - Claims Mode.
