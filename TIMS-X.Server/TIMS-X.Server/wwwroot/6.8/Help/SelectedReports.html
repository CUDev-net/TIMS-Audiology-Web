    Selected Reports

    All reports in the Selected category are generated only for the item that is currently selected. The required item for the report is in parentheses after the report name. For example, Appointment List (Patient) requires you to first highlight a patient name from the list before running the report from the Reports icon . It will then display all appointments for that patient only. Intake Sheet (Appt) would require you to first highlight an appointment on the appointment grid. The Selected reports are accessed from summary screens and panels, depending on which link you are in.  Only the applicable reports for that link will be displayed. Access the applicable selected reports from these locations:

    • Selected Patient - Select a patient from the Patients Panel, then click the click on the Reports/Letters/Captioned Telephone icon on the Patient Summary Panel,

    .

    • Click the Reports button . Available stock reports are:
      1. Advanced Beneficiary Notice (Patient) - form CMS-R-131
      2. Appointment List (Patient)  - A list of appointments for the selected patient.  Includes optional filter for appointment date range.
      3. Claim Transaction History (Patient) - A detailed list of all transmitted and open claims for the selected patient.  Useful for tracking down claims problems.
      4. Claim Transaction Journal (Patient) - A list of claim transactions for the selected patient and selected period.
      5. Hearing Aid Quote (Patient) - A form for quoting hearing aids, earmolds, accessories and other charges. 
      6. Intake Sheet (Patient) - A form used to collect demographic and insurance information from the patient and/or insured person.  Any current information from the database will print in the applicable fields, leaving the rest blank to be filled in by the patient.
      7. Loaner History (Patient) - A list of all outstanding and returned loaners for the selected patient.
      8. Outstanding Claims (Patient) - Details of transmitted and open claims for the selected patient. Summarized by payer and claim.
      9. Past Claims and Diagnosis (Patient) - Summary of past procedures and diagnosis codes submitted for the selected patient.
      10. Patient Activity Report (Patient) - A list of all visits for the selected patient, including Appointments, HAHistory, History, Repair, and Battery Club.  Sorted in descending order of date seen.
      11. QB Statement (Patient) - Statement based on QuickBooks balances for selected patient.
      12. Repairs History (Patient) - A listing of all repairs for the selected patient.
    • Selected Appointment - Select an appointment on the Appointment Grid, then click the Reports icon on the Appointment Summary Panel. Selected patient reports can also be run from here.
      1. Intake Sheet (Appt) - A form used to collect demographic and insurance information from the patient and/or insured person. Any current information from the database will print in the applicable fields, leaving the rest blank to be filled in by the patient. Select an appointment first in the Appointments Link.
      2. Super Bill (Appt) - A form used for recording details of a patient's appointment, such as procedures performed, diagnosis, charges, and notes.  Can be used to enter transaction information in the Transactions Link.
    • Selected HA History - Select a hearing aid history or Repair from the HA History link, then click the Reports icon on either the HAHistory summary or the Repairs and L&D section. Selected patient reports can also be run from here.

    Repairs

      1. Repair Form (HAHistory) - A form designed to be sent out to the repair facility, with the hearing aid repair. Displays patient, instrument and repair details.  Allows for input of repair facility name and account number. Select the desired repair from the repair list.
    • Selected History - there are no stock selected History reports, but custom ones may be added for your practice. Select a history from the History tree, click the Reports icon in the Patient Summary Panel, then click the Reports button . Selected patient reports can also be run from here.
    • Selected POS - open a POS document from either the Patient Activity Panel or the POS button from the Patient Summary Panel. If the document has been finalized, the Documents button will be active. Click this button to run both selected POS (such as purchase agreements) or selected patient reports.
      1. Bill of Sale (POS Invoice) - similar to the invoice form, but has a slightly different format.  Displays the details of the selected POS Invoice.  Note:  the invoice must be finalized before this report can be run.
      2. Invoice History (POS Invoice) - Displays details of selected finalized invoice, including all payments and credits applied to it and remaining TIMS balance.
      3. Purchase Agreement (POS Invoice) - Purchase Agreement for the selected POS Invoice. Note: the invoice must be finalized before this report can be run.   Note: Purchase Agreements (and Hearing Aid Contracts) are created custom for each practice to meet their needs. There is no "stock" agreement in TIMS.  Contact Support for details. 


    Any Selected report has the option of being saved in the patient's Activity History. After generating the report, click the Print icon . A message will appear, "Do you wish to archive this document?" Choose Yes, then the print screen will appear. After printing, the report will appear in the Patient Activity Panel , under "Archived Reports".  You can also archive the report directly without printing by clicking the Archive report button.


    Signature Reports

    Selected reports also have the option to include a place for the patient to add a signature.  Signatures are collected using the supported Topaz® signature pad. (contact TIMS Support for details)  If a report has been set to include a signature, the following message will pop up as soon as it is run:


    If the you are the patient wishes to see the full report before signing, click the Preview button. Then close the report to return to the Signature Required screen.  Have the patient sign on the Topaz® signature pad, then click the green Save button. 

    The signature will then appear in the designated spot on the report. Note: the patient's signature is not saved anywhere in the database, only as a snapshot on the printed and/or archived report.
