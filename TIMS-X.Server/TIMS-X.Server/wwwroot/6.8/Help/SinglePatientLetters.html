  1. Communications

    Single Patient Letters

    Letter templates for single patient use are created in the same way as for bulk letters. See the Creating and Editing Letters help for details.  When creating a letter in the Communications link, it can be designated as a Single Patient letter at that time.


    Once the letter has been created, it can be accessed from the Patient Summary Panel.

    1. Select any patient, then in the Patient Summary panel click the Reports/Letters/Captioned Telephone icon .
    2. Click the Letters button .
    3. Select the desired letter from the letter list, then click the OK button.

    1. Depending on which merge fields have been included in your letter, you may be prompted to select the data you want to include. Only one record can be selected.

    In this example, the letter includes History details, so before the letter can be displayed you must select the particular history you want to use for the letter. Choose the appropriate data, then click the green OK button.

    1. The letter will be displayed.  You can edit the letter at this point if desired. You can enter additional text, change formatting, insert graphics, etc.  These changes will be printed and saved with any archive.

    1. If the letter contains any signature fields to be signed electronically, checkboxes, or date pickers:
      1. Signature Fields - click on the field on the screen. The Signature Required screen will popup.

    Have the patient sign on the signature pad, then click the green OK button.  To get a new signature, click Clear and try again. Or click Cancel to not collect the signature. The signature will now be embedded in the document. Note: the signature is not saved anywhere in the database, only as a snapshot on the printed and/or archived document.

     

      1. Checkboxes - click on the box once to highlight it, then again to check the box.  Click on it again to un-check the box if needed.

      1. Date picker - click on the box.  A date selection screen will popup. Click once on the desired date, changing the month if needed. If the date needs to be changed, click on the field again to re-select a new date.

    1. Once the letter has been generated and edited as needed, it can be printed, emailed, faxed, or archived from the File menu.

      1. Print - click to open the print window. Select your printer then click Print.
      2. Email - this choice will only be active if an Email Subject has been entered for the letter, and your practice has been setup for TIMS Email Service.  (fees applicable - contact TIMS Support at 800-763-0308 for details) The email panel will appear to the right of the report. Letters can be emailed to physicians with a secure email address entered, the patient, or a manually entered one-time email address. Multiple addresses can be emailed at the same time (separately). The patient's primary care and referring physician will be selected automatically.  These can be un-checked, and/or a different physician can be checked if needed.  The email Subject can also be changed as needed.  Check or enter the email recipients then click the green Send button. A message will popup to confirm the number of recipients, click Yes to send or No to cancel.

      1. Fax - this choice will only be active if your practice has been setup for TIMS Fax Service. (fees applicable - contact TIMS Support at 800-763-0308 for details) The fax panel will appear to the right of the report. Letters can be faxed to physicians or a manually entered one-time fax number. The procedure is the same as with Diagnostic Reports. Multiple faxes can be sent at the same time. The patient's primary care and referring physician will be selected automatically. If the physician has multiple fax numbers, they will be listed under the "Main Fax". Click the down arrow to select an alternate number. If a Cover Page has been selected, you must enter something in Subject.  Comments are optional. Click the Preview button to see what the cover page will look like.  Sample information will be displayed for the from and to information, but the subject and comments will show as entered. Click Cancel to close the preview. Click Send Fax to send the fax, or Cancel to abandon. A confirmation message will popup. Click Yes to proceed, or No to cancel.

      1. Archive - save a snapshot of the letter to the Archived Letters folder in Patient Activity.
    1. Depending on the letter's setting for Auto Archive, a message may popup asking if you wish to archive a copy of the letter after you've printed, emailed, or faxed it and then close the window. Click Yes to archive or No to close without archiving.
    2. You can view and/or re-print a copy of the archived letter at any time by double-clicking on it under Archived Letters.  Any re-prints will be recorded in the Print History list to the right of the letter.
